    • How it works

    Three local pieces, zero cloud. Everything runs as fast as your Mac does.

    1

    It hears the whole call

    Oatmeal captures system audio (their side) and your microphone (your side) natively on macOS — Zoom, Meet, Teams, anything that makes sound. It can even notice a meeting starting and offer to take notes.

    2

    Transcribes on-device

    Whisper runs locally with Metal acceleration. Pick your model in Settings — fast & light or maximum accuracy — and download it with one click. Speakers are labeled Me / Them automatically.

    3

    Summarizes & answers live

    Your own Ollama model keeps a rolling summary — TL;DR, decisions, action items — updating as the meeting happens. Afterwards (or during), ask the transcript anything.

    Private by physics, not by policy

    There is no server to trust. The audio pipeline literally has nowhere to send your data.

    ✓Audio stays on disk

    Capture, transcription, storage — all in ~/Library on your Mac. Nothing is uploaded, ever.

    ✓Bring your own models

    Whisper for ears, any Ollama model for brains. Swap them anytime; no vendor, no API key, no metering.

    ✓Works offline

    On a plane, in a SCIF, on hotel wifi — once models are downloaded, Oatmeal needs zero connectivity.

    Your models, your tradeoffs

    Sensible defaults, one-click downloads, or point it at anything you already run in Ollama.

    Ears

    Whisper base.en (fast) · small.en (default) · large-v3-turbo (most accurate, multilingual). Downloaded in-app, Metal-accelerated.

    Brains

    Anything in Ollama: qwen2.5:14b for quality, llama3.2:3b for speed on smaller Macs. Oatmeal lists what's installed and pulls what isn't.

    Rule of thumb

    16 GB Macs: base.en + a 3B model. 24 GB and up: small.en + a 14B model runs comfortably alongside your call.

    Requirements

    Honest list — it's a local AI app, it needs a modern Mac.

    • macOS13 Ventura or newer
    • ChipApple Silicon (M1 or newer)
    • Memory16 GB+ recommended
    • Ollamafree, for summaries & chat — ollama.com
    • PermissionsMicrophone + Screen Recording (for system audio)
    “Oatmeal can't be opened” on first launch?

    Oatmeal is open source and currently unsigned (no $99 Apple Developer certificate). On first launch, right-click the app → Open → Open. Or from Terminal: xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/Oatmeal.app. You only need to do this once.
